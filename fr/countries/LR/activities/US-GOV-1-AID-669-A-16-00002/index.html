Forest Incomes for Environmental Sustainability (FIFES)

Forest Incomes for Environmental Sustainability (FIFES) project's overall goal is to develop key rural forest-based enterprises that provide inclusive, sustainable economic opportunities for rural farmers and forest-dependent communities in a way that also combats deforestation and biodiversity loss. Project Objectives: Strengthen selected forest and agricultural value chains; Establish legal and management frameworks for forest enterprises; and Enhance knowledge and skills for forest enterprises and landscape management.

Objectives for Liberia: Planned Outcomes: Monitor beneficiaries to assess their awareness and understanding of forest biodiversity as well as monitor the capacity of local partners and organizations to provide support for forest-dependent communities.; Work with 11 community forests to improve the surrounding local communities’ ability to manage the forests in a sustainable and economically beneficial manner through increasing the productivity of existing value chains and promoting good governance of the management bodies for each forest.; In 5-10 years, only community based entrepreneurs, working with the Community Forest, and using local labor, sustainably harvest and mill (either directly, or through agreements) timber harvested from community forests and other community lands on a pre-order basis for buyers in urban centers. No outside actors harvest community timber, and no community members harvest trees without Community Forest approval.
