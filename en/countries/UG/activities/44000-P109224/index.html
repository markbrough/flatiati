Agricultural Technology and Agrribusiness Advisory Services

The objective of the Agricultural Technology and Agribusiness Advisory Services Project (ATAAS) for Uganda is to increase agricultural productivity and incomes of participating households by improving the performance of agricultural research and advisory service systems in the Republic of Uganda. As part of government reforms, the agricultural extension mandate has been transferred from the original agency providing advisory services under the ATAAS project to the Ministry. In addition, the project included technology uptake grants that were intended to support technology demonstrations and multiplication. Government has decided to re-orient and significantly scale these grants into a national mechanism for distribution of agricultural inputs to farmers outside the ATAAS. In view of these developments the following changes are proposed: (i) modifications to project components and activities especially those that were originally the remit of NAADS; (ii) revisions to the results framework to match the revised scope of project activities; (iii) changes to the institutional and implementation arrangements; and extension of the closing date from June 30, 2016 to December 31, 2017 to allow completion of ongoing and proposed new activities.
